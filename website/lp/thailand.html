Questions from Thai Customers

Common Questions

What is the import duty on mold inserts from China to Thailand?

Under the ASEAN-China Free Trade Agreement (ACFTA), most metal mold components (HS code 8480.71 or 8480.79) qualify for 0% import duty from China to Thailand. You need to request a Certificate of Origin (Form E) from us, which we provide at no charge. This is a significant cost advantage over sourcing from Europe or Japan. Confirm with your customs broker for your specific HS code.

Do you have experience supplying Japanese or Korean-owned factories in Thailand?

Yes. We supply several Toyota and Honda tier-1 and tier-2 suppliers in the Eastern Seaboard region. Our quality documentation (CMM reports, material certificates, hardness test data, pressure test records) meets the QC requirements typical of Japanese OEM supply chains. We understand the importance of zero-defect delivery and provide full traceability documentation with each shipment.

Can I order a sample before committing to a larger quantity?

Absolutely. We accept single-piece orders at no premium. Most Thai customers start with 1–2 sample inserts to test dimensional fit and cooling performance before placing production orders. We recommend a sample trial even when you're confident in the design — it confirms real-world fit before investing in multiple inserts.

What payment methods do you accept for Thai customers?

We accept T/T (bank wire transfer), PayPal, and Wise. Standard terms are 50% advance to start production, 50% balance before shipping. For repeat customers with established track record, we offer 30-day net payment terms. We do not use Thai agents or distributors — you deal directly with our factory team in Ningbo.

My mold was designed by a Japanese engineer — can you match the quality and tolerances?

Yes. We routinely produce to ±0.01 mm on mating surfaces and ±0.02 mm on cavity surfaces, which matches or exceeds Japanese toolroom standards. Our CNC machining is done on Makino and DMG Mori equipment. Surface roughness Ra 0.4–0.8 μm on cavity surfaces is standard. We provide a CMM report with every insert so your quality department can verify all critical dimensions.
