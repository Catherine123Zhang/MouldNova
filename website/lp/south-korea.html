Korea-China FTA: How It Works for Your Business

Save Up to 8% Import Duty with KCFTA Form K

Under the Korea-China Free Trade Agreement (KCFTA), mould inserts and tooling components manufactured in China can qualify for a preferential FTA duty rate of 0–3% on HS 8480.71 (injection moulds for rubber/plastics), compared to the standard MFN rate of 8%. To claim the KCFTA rate, your Korean customs broker requires a Certificate of Origin (Form K) issued by the Chinese exporter.

We provide Certificate of Origin (Form K) with every qualifying shipment to South Korea, enabling your business to access the reduced FTA duty rate automatically.

Korean VAT (부가가치세, 10%) applies on the CIF import value. For VAT-registered businesses, import VAT is fully recoverable as an input tax credit on your VAT return — making the effective net VAT cost zero for most Korean manufacturing companies.

With KCFTA Form K + VAT input credit: effective net import cost is 0–3% duty only.

Pressure Integrity

Hydrostatic Pressure Certificate

Every cooling insert undergoes a hydrostatic pressure test before shipping. Test pressure, hold duration, and pass confirmation are recorded on a signed hydrostatic pressure certificate. Standard test: 150 bar / 15 minutes. This eliminates the risk of channel leaks once the insert is assembled in your tool — a critical quality gate for high-volume automotive and battery component production where downtime costs are severe.

Surface Quality

Ra Measurement on All Sealing Faces

Surface roughness (Ra) is measured and recorded on all sealing faces and mating surfaces using a contact profilometer. Ra values and measurement locations are documented. This ensures correct sealing face finish for O-ring or metal-to-metal seals and provides the material traceability that Korean automotive and electronics toolmakers require for supplier qualification.

Why Conformal Cooling Works for Automotive Sensor Housings

A 32-cavity ABS automotive sensor housing is a demanding application. The complex geometry — thin walls, integral connector features, and tight positional tolerances for PCB mounting — creates non-uniform heat distribution across the cavity surface. Conventional straight-drill cooling leaves persistent hot spots at ribs, bosses, and corner radii, lengthening the required cooling phase and causing dimensional drift across the shot.

Conformal cooling channels that follow the exact contour of the housing walls maintain a uniform temperature delta across the entire cavity. This reduces the hottest-zone temperature, shortens the cooling phase, and eliminates the dimensional variation that would otherwise require rework or trigger an IATF 16949 NCR with the OEM.

At 600,000 parts per year across a 32-cavity tool, a 21% cycle reduction equals $58,000 in annual machine-time savings. The conformal insert set at $2,800 pays back in approximately 17 working days — one of the fastest ROI profiles in precision tooling.
