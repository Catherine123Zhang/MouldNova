Common Questions

For Product Companies New to Injection Molding

Do I need to know anything about mold-making to order from you?
No. You bring your 3D product file (STEP or IGES). We handle mold design, steel selection, insert manufacturing, trial shots, and first-article inspection. You approve the sample and we begin production. Our team will guide you through each decision in plain English.
How fast can I get sample plastic parts?
First trial shots are typically available within 15–20 business days from design lock. Rush service (10 days) is available for simple geometries. For reference: a conventional mold from a Chinese toolmaker usually takes 25–35 days just to build the mold, before any trial shots.
What's the minimum order quantity?
There is no minimum. We regularly run 50–500 piece sample runs for product companies before they scale. Once the mold is built, it's yours — you can use it for future production runs at your pace.
What plastic materials do you support?
PP, ABS, PC, Nylon (PA6/PA66), POM, PEEK, TPU, TPE and most engineering plastics. We advise on material selection for your product requirements — temperature range, regulatory compliance (FDA, RoHS, etc.), mechanical strength, and budget all factor in.
Can you also make the metal parts that go with my plastic product?
Yes. Our in-house CNC machining and EDM handles aluminum, stainless steel, and titanium parts. We coordinate plastic and metal components in the same shipment — no need to manage multiple suppliers and reconcile separate deliveries.
What if the first sample isn't right?
If the sample doesn't meet the agreed drawing spec, we re-make the affected tooling or adjust process parameters at our cost. We've never had a customer stuck with a wrong part — our DFM review catches most issues before manufacturing begins.
How do I pay? What payment terms are available?
Standard terms: 50% deposit, 50% before shipment. We accept T/T bank transfer (USD, EUR, GBP). For established repeat customers, we offer net-30 on production orders. All pricing is in USD and includes a detailed cost breakdown by line item.
