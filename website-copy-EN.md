### [INTRO]

**What Is Conformal Cooling?**

Conformal cooling is a mold cooling method in which the cooling channels follow (or "conform to") the contour of the molded part, rather than running in straight lines as in conventional cooling.

Because the channels are positioned close to the mold surface at every point, heat is extracted more uniformly and efficiently — resulting in:
- Shorter cooling time (by 15–72%)
- More uniform mold temperature
- Reduced part warpage and dimensional variation
- Elimination of surface defects like gate burn marks
- Lower unit production cost
- Extended mold insert service life

Conformal cooling channels cannot be manufactured by conventional drilling. **They require metal 3D printing (additive manufacturing)** — which is exactly what we do.

---

### [HOW IT WORKS — 5 steps]

**Step 1 — Part Analysis**
We receive your part drawing or existing mold design. Our engineers analyze the geometry, identify hot spots, and determine the optimal cooling channel layout.

**Step 2 — Cooling Channel Design**
Using NX or SolidWorks, we design internal cooling channels that closely follow your part surface. We can design multiple channels simultaneously, including double-helix configurations for cylindrical cores.

**Step 3 — Simulation & Validation**
We run Moldex3D thermal simulation to validate cooling time, temperature uniformity, and potential warpage — before printing.

**Step 4 — Metal 3D Printing**
The insert is printed in 420 mold steel or 18Ni300 maraging steel using selective laser melting (SLM). Hardness reaches 50–55 HRC after heat treatment.

**Step 5 — Post-Processing & Delivery**
Heat treatment, support removal, CNC finishing, polishing to your required surface finish (up to SPI-A1). Then packed and shipped to your facility.

## SEO PAGE: WHAT IS CONFORMAL COOLING? (Blog / Resource Page)

**[H1]**
What Is Conformal Cooling? A Complete Guide for Injection Molders

**[META DESCRIPTION]**
Conformal cooling uses 3D-printed channels that follow your part geometry to cool injection molds faster and more evenly. Learn how it works, its advantages, and real performance data.

---

### Introduction
Conformal cooling is a mold cooling technique in which the cooling channels are designed to follow the contour of the molded part — rather than running in straight lines as in conventional molds.

The word "conformal" means "following the shape of." In a conformal cooling system, the water channels are positioned as close as possible to the mold cavity surface at every point, regardless of the part's geometry.

This is only possible with **metal 3D printing (additive manufacturing)**, because traditional drilling can only produce straight holes.

---

### Conformal Cooling vs Conventional Cooling

| | Conventional Cooling | Conformal Cooling |
|---|---|---|
| Channel shape | Straight (drilled) | Follows part geometry |
| Manufacturing method | CNC drilling | Metal 3D printing |
| Cooling uniformity | Uneven — hot spots remain | Even — full surface coverage |
| Cycle time | Baseline | 15–72% shorter |
| Part warpage | Common in complex shapes | Significantly reduced |
| Mold temperature | Often unstable | Stable, controllable |
| Gate burn marks | Common with PETG/MS | Eliminated |
| Mold lifespan | Standard | Extended (consistent thermal load) |

---

### Advantages of Conformal Cooling

**1. Shorter cycle time**
By removing heat uniformly from all surfaces simultaneously, conformal cooling allows the part to reach ejection temperature much faster. Real-world data from our projects shows improvements of 15–72%.

**2. Better part quality**
Uneven cooling is the primary cause of warpage, sink marks, and residual stress in injection-molded parts. Conformal cooling eliminates the temperature gradients that cause these defects.

**3. No more gate burn marks**
For transparent materials (PETG, MS, PC), burn marks at the gate are a common problem when mold temperatures are too high. Conformal cooling keeps the gate area cool, eliminating this issue.

**4. Higher production output**
Shorter cycle time directly increases parts per hour from the same machine. Our customers have seen output increases of 15–73%.

**5. Lower unit cost**
More parts per hour + fewer defects = lower cost per good part. Reductions of ¥0.11–¥3.60 per unit have been achieved across our case studies.

**6. Longer mold life**
Consistent, controlled cooling reduces thermal fatigue and extends insert service life.

---

### How Is Conformal Cooling Made?

Conformal cooling channels cannot be manufactured by conventional subtractive machining (drilling, milling) because they are internal, curved, and complex.

**Metal 3D printing (selective laser melting / SLM)** builds the insert layer by layer from metal powder, allowing any internal channel geometry to be created. The process at Saiguang 3D:

1. Cooling channel design in NX / SolidWorks
2. Thermal simulation in Moldex3D
3. Support structure design in Magics
4. SLM printing in 420 steel or 18Ni300 maraging steel
5. Heat treatment to 50–55 HRC
6. CNC finishing to final dimensions
7. Polishing to SPI-A1 if required

### Is Conformal Cooling Right for Your Mold?

Conformal cooling gives the greatest benefit when:
- Your part has deep ribs, bosses, or complex 3D geometry
- You're experiencing gate burn marks or surface defects
- Cycle time is limiting your production capacity
- You're molding transparent or optical-grade plastics (PETG, MS, PC)
- Part warpage is causing dimensional issues
- You're using a hot runner system
